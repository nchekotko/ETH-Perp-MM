# CMF Market Making Backtester — Report

## Abstract

This submission implements an event-driven market-making backtester from
scratch, calibrates the Avellaneda–Stoikov order-flow intensity and price
volatility on a 70/30 chronological train/test split of a 6-day crypto LOB
dataset (≈21.86M trades, ≈1.04M LOB snapshots), and compares three variants
of the Avellaneda–Stoikov (2008) strategy out-of-sample:

* **V1 — vanilla AS**, with mid-price reference.
* **V2 — AS with volume-weighted micro-price** as the reference.
* **V3 — AS with imbalance-driven asymmetric skew** (Cartea-style
  adverse-selection lean).

The headline P&L decomposition (spread capture vs inventory P&L) is
enforced as a class invariant and is the principal lens through which we
read the results.

## 1. Methodology

### 1.1 Architecture

The engine is a pull-style event loop:

1. The loader merges `trades.parquet` and `lob.parquet` into a single
   chronologically-sorted iterator of `TradeEvent | LOBEvent`. Trades are
   emitted before tied LOB updates (the trade hits the *pre-update* book).
2. On each LOB event the book updates and the running inventory P&L picks up
   `q · Δmid`.
3. On each trade event the matcher checks fills against active resting
   orders; the fill price is the resting limit price, not the trade's print.
4. The strategy emits a desired quote state; the order manager diffs it
   against active orders and reconciles via cancel/place pairs.

Execution rule (from the task spec):

* a resting **buy** at p fills when an incoming **sell-aggressor** trade
  prints at price ≤ p;
* a resting **sell** at p fills when an incoming **buy-aggressor** trade
  prints at price ≥ p.

Fills happen FIFO on submit timestamp. Partial fills are enabled in the
shipped configs.

### 1.2 Calibration

* **σ** — per-second standard deviation of mid log-returns:
  `σ² = mean(Δlog mid)² / Δt` over consecutive LOB snapshots.
  Calibrated value: **σ ≈ 3.02 × 10⁻⁴ / √s**.
* **(A, k)** — log-linear regression on the *density* per second per unit-δ:
  `log(rate / bin_width) = log A − k·δ`, weighted by √count, on log-spaced
  bins between the 1st and 99th percentiles of |trade − mid|. Calibrated:
  **A ≈ 1.60 × 10⁷, k ≈ 4.33 × 10⁵, R² = 0.648** on 10.1M observations.

Both are fit only on the train period and frozen for the test backtest.

### 1.3 Strategies

```
r(s, q, t) = s − q·γ·σ²·(T − t)
δ_a + δ_b  = γ·σ²·(T − t) + (2/γ)·log(1 + γ/k)
p_bid      = r − ½(δ_a + δ_b)
p_ask      = r + ½(δ_a + δ_b)
```

We use the **infinite-horizon limit** (`T_horizon_seconds = 0`, i.e.
`T − t = 1` second constant) — open-ended backtesting has no natural T,
and the rolling-1h variant introduces a discontinuity at hourly boundaries.

V2 substitutes `s → p_micro = (Q_b·P_a + Q_a·P_b) / (Q_a + Q_b)` everywhere.
V3 leans against imbalance to reduce adverse selection:

```
δ_a = δ* + α·I    (wider ask when I > 0)
δ_b = δ* − α·I    (tighter bid when I > 0)
```

Strategies do not cross the book: any quote that would price through the
opposite touch is clipped to one milli-spread inside it.

## 2. Results

Test split is the last 30% of the dataset (~1.8 days, 9.52M trades). Same
calibration applied to all three strategies; quote refresh throttle 100 ms;
order size 100; max inventory 5000; γ = 200.

| Strategy        | Total PnL | Spread capture | Inventory P&L | Inv std | N fills |
|---|---:|---:|---:|---:|---:|
| V1 vanilla      | −4.965    | +1.376         | −6.341        | 29.96   | 52,111  |
| V2 micro-price  | −4.961    | +1.376         | −6.338        | 29.92   | 52,133  |
| V3 asymmetric   | −14.74    | −0.264         | −14.48        | 45.34   | 154,629 |

Figures generated by `make report` are in `results/report/`:

* `pnl_comparison.png` — total PnL over the test period for V1, V2, V3.
* `decomposition_bars.png` — spread capture vs inventory P&L by strategy.
* `intensity_calibration.png` — log-rate vs δ scatter with the fitted line.
* `summary.md` and `summary.tex` — comparative metrics tables.

### 2.1 What the numbers say

The decomposition is the cleanest read on what's happening:

* V1/V2 capture **+1.38 of spread** but **lose −6.34 to inventory P&L**.
  The strategy is profitable on the "edge" component but is repeatedly
  caught long going down or short going up. This is textbook AS adverse
  selection: the model treats arrivals as Poisson with constant intensity,
  which understates the conditional probability of a follow-up move.
* V2 vs V1: identical to four significant figures on every metric. The
  micro-price deviation `(p_micro − mid)` has an empirical mean magnitude
  of **5.2 × 10⁻⁸**, an order of magnitude smaller than the AS-prescribed
  half-spread of **1.15 × 10⁻⁵** at γ = 200. With the AS quote sitting far
  outside the touch, the small reference-price shift V2 introduces does not
  meaningfully change which trades fill us.
* V3 (α = 5×10⁻⁶, leaning against imbalance) buys more when the book is
  bid-heavy and sells more when ask-heavy. This roughly triples the fill
  count but worsens spread capture (−0.26) and compounds inventory
  exposure. The adverse-selection lean only pays off when the imbalance
  signal *correctly* predicts mid moves a non-trivial fraction of the time;
  this dataset's top-of-book imbalance is too noisy at the 100 ms refresh
  cadence we use here.

### 2.2 The regime issue

The natural top-of-book spread on this dataset has a mean of **1.7 × 10⁻⁷**
(price units, on mid ≈ 8.8 × 10⁻³). The AS-prescribed half-spread at the
calibrated (σ, k) is bounded below by `1/k ≈ 2.3 × 10⁻⁶`, which is **27×
the entire book spread**. AS is therefore telling us "do not quote at the
touch" on this asset — the volatility-to-arrival-rate ratio is too high
for the model's risk-aversion logic to find a quoting price that beats
holding cash.

All three strategies are in this off-touch regime; fills happen because
the data occasionally prints multi-tick trades that sweep through to our
deeper resting orders (and partial fills are enabled). The headline result
is that, in this regime, **the micro-price reference is operationally
indistinguishable from the mid**, exactly as the algebra predicts.

## 3. Discussion

### 3.1 Limitations

* **No queue position.** Resting orders fill the moment a trade prints at
  their level. This is optimistic; with realistic queue-position modelling
  (roadmap §4) the V1/V2 numbers would shift.
* **No latency.** Quote submit/cancel is instantaneous in the model.
* **Single-asset.** No cross-asset hedging.
* **Static (γ, σ, k).** Walk-forward calibration is on the small-items
  list of the roadmap.
* **Aggressor side trusted.** The dataset provides "buy"/"sell" aggressor
  labels directly; we do not re-derive via tick rule.

### 3.2 Why micro-price doesn't help here

Micro-price helps exactly when the AS quote sits close to the touch and
the typical `|p_micro − mid|` is comparable to or larger than the AS
half-spread. On this dataset the AS half-spread is 200× larger than the
typical micro-price deviation, so V2's reference-price shift is below the
fill quantum. The "right" experiment to surface a V2 advantage is to (a)
quote at the touch (drop the AS half-spread and use a fixed offset), or
(b) use a dataset with much higher volatility relative to arrival decay.
Both are extensions, not changes to V2 itself.

The mathematically clean V1↔V2 comparison is preserved — the same
calibration, same execution, same parameters. The negative result is
informative: the micro-price advantage is regime-dependent and the AS
calibration on this asset is in the wrong regime to expose it.

### 3.3 What would change the picture

* **Drop AS half-spread, quote at touch ± 1 tick** (`fixed_spread` strategy).
  Then V2's micro-price effect would dominate the quote location.
* **Tune γ via the sweep** to push half-spread closer to the natural book
  spread, keeping the AS theoretical structure but with tighter fills.
* **Use multi-level depth** (full L2) instead of top-of-book micro-price.

These are framed as extensions in the roadmap rather than changes to the
present submission, which is deliberately restricted to the canonical AS
formulation to make the V1↔V2 comparison apples-to-apples.
